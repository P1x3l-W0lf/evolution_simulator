# Evolution Simulator
An evolution simulator where you can watch little pixel creatures adapt to their environment over generations. It is a browser based, visual representation of natural selection, and you can watch the critters [right here](https://p1x3l-w0lf.github.io/evolution_simulator/). 

*P.S. they look more lifelike on a higher speed!*

I built this with vanilla JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. I'm very proud of it. This is my first smooth experience making a (relatively) bigger project, and I think I've learned a lot!

## Instructions
There's not much to it; Just open the link in your browser and watch the pixels go!

The slider at the top of the page adjusts how fast the simulation progresses, with 1 being the slowest and 10 being the fastest. If you would like to generate a new environment and restart the simulation just refresh the page.

If, for some reason, you want to run it locally, just clone this repo and run from dev.

## Key to the Colors
There are 5 unique colors that you can find in my simulator:

  - Red. This is for food sources.
  - Light Blue. This is for water sources.
  - Dark Blue. This is for male pixel creatures.
  - Pink. This is for female pixel creatures.
  - Green. This is for disease cells.

Food and water sources have static positions and also take up more space than any of the other things.

The pixel creatures will wander around looking for food, water, and potential mates. (sidenote: I made it so that the males' were the ones who had the babies, just like seahorses.)

Disease cells only show up if the population starts to get too dense. They will hunt down pixel creatures and kill any that they touch.
